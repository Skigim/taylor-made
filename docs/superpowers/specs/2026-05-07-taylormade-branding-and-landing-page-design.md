# TaylorMade LLC Branding and Landing Page Design

## Overview

TaylorMade LLC is a premium modernization partner for small local businesses, starting with salons and barbers. The company helps owners replace scattered, hard-to-manage tools with a more coherent operating system across online presence, booking, billing, scheduling, reminders, and related customer workflows.

This design defines the initial branding package and the first landing page experience. The goal is to position TaylorMade as a calm, capable, consultative partner that can assess an existing setup, identify operational friction, and implement right-fit infrastructure without sounding like a generic agency or a trendy SaaS brand.

## Audience and Positioning

### Primary Audience

- Appointment-based local businesses
- Initial focus: salons and barber shops
- Owner-operators and small teams that need stronger systems without enterprise complexity

### Primary Offer

- Free business systems audit

### Positioning

TaylorMade should combine these two positions:

- Full-service partner: someone who can handle the systems side of a local business end-to-end
- Practical modernization specialist: someone who makes a shop easier to run and easier to buy from

The brand should not feel like a broad marketing agency. It should feel like a trusted operator who understands service businesses and can simplify the infrastructure behind them.

## Brand Strategy

### Core Concept

The name TaylorMade works because it communicates both custom fit and personal ownership. The brand promise is not generic service delivery. It is tailored business infrastructure designed around how a specific shop actually runs.

### Brand Promise

TaylorMade builds custom-fit business systems that help local service businesses operate more smoothly, capture more appointments, and present themselves more professionally.

### Mission Statement

To provide highly customized, high-quality business infrastructure that is carefully designed to fit the exact needs, workflows, and growth goals of each client.

### Core Values

- Precision: careful attention to details that affect the client and owner experience
- Personalization: no one-size-fits-all setup or templated recommendations
- Quality: premium execution across strategy, design, implementation, and communication
- Authenticity: direct, honest, consultative relationships with clients

## Brand Personality and Voice

### Personality

TaylorMade should feel:

- Calm
- Competent
- Premium
- Practical
- Personal
- Trustworthy

The overall impression should be “quiet authority.” The company should feel polished and confident without sounding flashy, overly technical, or creatively self-indulgent.

### Voice Rules

- Consultative, not pushy
- Confident, not boastful
- Clear, not jargon-heavy
- Personal, not overly casual
- Outcome-driven, not feature-dumped

### Copy Guidance

Copy should focus on business friction and operational clarity rather than abstract creativity.

Use language like:

- We build the systems that make your shop easier to run.
- Your business deserves better than a patchwork of tools.
- We audit the gaps, prioritize the fixes, and build the setup around how you actually work.

Avoid language that sounds:

- like a generic growth agency
- overly salesy or urgent
- startup-cliche heavy
- luxury for luxury’s sake

## Visual Identity

### Approved Direction

The approved direction is:

- Direction A structure and posture: trusted operator
- Direction B palette influence: richer, more atmospheric color treatment

This means the identity should feel composed and premium, with stronger visual depth than a flat corporate site.

### Visual Tone

The visual system should communicate:

- premium trust
- operational clarity
- bespoke care
- local-business credibility

It should not resemble:

- bright SaaS landing pages
- minimalist luxury brands disconnected from business outcomes
- generic corporate consulting templates

## Logo System

### Primary Logo

Use a wordmark-first approach.

- Primary mark: refined serif wordmark for TaylorMade
- The wordmark should feel editorial and precise, not ornamental
- “LLC” may appear in formal business contexts but should not be required in the main marketing presentation

### Secondary Logo

Use a secondary monogram for utility contexts.

- `TM` monogram
- Suitable for favicon, profile image, social avatar, compact stamp, and small UI placements
- May sit inside a square or softly geometric frame

### Graphic Device

Use a thin gold line or precision rule as a recurring accent motif.

This device can appear:

- under headings
- beside labels
- in cards
- inside the logo lockup system when appropriate

## Color Palette

The palette should keep the approved luxury-business tone while staying grounded and readable.

### Core Colors

- Ink Navy: primary dark background and hero color
- Deep Steel: supporting blue-gray for surfaces, panels, and borders
- Warm Champagne: premium accent for highlights, rules, buttons, and emphasis
- Soft Alabaster: warm light background for content sections and negative space

### Color Intent

- Dark colors establish trust, depth, and seriousness
- Gold is used sparingly as emphasis, not as a dominant field color
- Light surfaces should feel warm and paper-like instead of stark white

### Usage Rules

- Hero and closing CTA can use dark ink backgrounds
- Main content sections should alternate between soft warm neutrals and deep panels when needed
- Gold should highlight actions, section dividers, and small moments of emphasis
- Avoid overusing gold, which would reduce the composed, premium feel

## Typography

### Heading Typeface

Preferred heading families:

- Playfair Display
- Cormorant Garamond
- Cinzel can be explored for logo-only or display use if needed

Headings should convey authority, polish, and a slight editorial quality.

### Body and UI Typeface

Preferred supporting families:

- Montserrat
- Manrope
- Lato is acceptable if needed for compatibility

Body typography should feel clean, digital, and highly readable.

### Typography Rules

- Serif for headlines, brand statements, and section titles
- Sans-serif for body copy, service explanations, labels, form fields, and buttons
- Use strong hierarchy with generous spacing
- Avoid overly compressed or decorative combinations

## Imagery and Art Direction

### Photography Direction

Imagery should show real service-business environments and the details of professional work.

Focus on:

- salons and barber stations
- hands at work
- tools and detail shots
- checkout or booking touchpoints
- comfortable client interactions
- screens or business tools used in realistic context

### Lighting and Mood

- warm, natural, directional lighting
- grounded, tactile, professional settings
- no harsh artificial stock-photo lighting

### Avoid

- staged startup team photos
- generic office collaboration imagery
- exaggerated tech visuals
- empty luxury imagery with no operational relevance

## Graphic System

The interface and brand system should use a restrained architectural rhythm.

### Core Elements

- thin dividing rules
- framed content areas
- numbered steps
- large editorial headings
- generous spacing
- selective use of cards rather than card overload

### Surface Treatment

- warm paper-like light backgrounds
- dark, atmospheric hero surfaces
- subtle texture or tonal depth where appropriate
- sharp, intentional alignment

## Landing Page Goals

The first landing page should serve as a focused offer page for the free systems audit.

### Primary Goal

Drive audit inquiries from salon and barber business owners.

### Secondary Goals

- establish trust
- clarify what TaylorMade actually does
- show that the service is practical and custom-fit
- make future service expansion possible without changing the core brand language

## Landing Page Narrative

The page should tell a simple story:

1. Your shop is dealing with more operational friction than it should.
2. The issue is usually a disconnected system, not lack of effort.
3. TaylorMade audits the current setup and identifies what to fix first.
4. TaylorMade can then build the right-fit infrastructure across presence, operations, and retention.

## Landing Page Structure

### 1. Hero Section

Purpose:

- establish the brand promise quickly
- describe the practical business value
- present the audit CTA immediately

Hero content should include:

- company name
- one strong headline
- one short explanatory paragraph
- primary CTA for the free systems audit
- a brief audience cue showing that the page is for appointment-based local businesses

Example headline direction:

Modern infrastructure for service businesses that deserve better systems.

### 2. Audit Scope Panel

Purpose:

- make the free audit feel concrete
- reduce vagueness around the offer

Suggested scope categories:

- online presence
- scheduling flow
- payments and operations

This panel should sit close to the hero so the offer feels specific and credible.

### 3. Problem Section

Purpose:

- name the operational pain in plain language
- show understanding of the client’s day-to-day reality

Example themes:

- missed calls become missed bookings
- clients face inconsistent websites and booking tools
- reminders, intake, billing, or follow-up are still manual

### 4. Capabilities Section

Purpose:

- show breadth without becoming vague
- group services into memorable buckets

Recommended service groups:

- Presence: website, listings, messaging, brand trust
- Operations: scheduling, reminders, intake, billing, workflow cleanup
- Retention: rebooking prompts, follow-up systems, customer touchpoints

### 5. Process Section

Purpose:

- make the engagement feel understandable and low-risk

Recommended steps:

1. Audit
2. Prioritize
3. Build

Each step should be short, concrete, and practical.

### 6. Closing CTA Section

Purpose:

- restate the offer
- convert users who needed more context before acting

This section should return to a dark background treatment and repeat the audit CTA with a concise value statement.

## Content Rules for the Landing Page

### Messaging Principles

- Lead with business pain and business clarity
- Keep technical details behind plain-language explanations
- Tie every service area to outcomes or reduced friction
- Keep custom language grounded in operations rather than artistry alone

### CTA Rules

Primary CTA:

- Get a free systems audit

Secondary CTA options can exist later, but the first version should be disciplined around one main action.

### Copy Examples

Possible hero line:

- You run the shop. TaylorMade builds the systems that make it easier to book, bill, and grow.

Possible audit framing line:

- A free business systems audit for salons and barbers who are tired of piecing together websites, booking tools, payments, and follow-up by hand.

## Implementation Notes

### Design Priorities

- strong hero composition
- clean section rhythm
- fewer, more intentional content blocks
- consistent spacing and alignment
- restrained use of accent color

### Responsiveness

The implementation should preserve the premium, editorial feel on mobile without collapsing into cramped cards or stacked clutter. The layout should simplify cleanly and keep hierarchy intact.

### Fine-Tuning During Build

The content structure is approved, but visual refinement will continue during implementation. The page should be treated as composition-led, not template-led.
